About MIFA

MIFA was founded in 1992 by local residents to create opportunities for all people in Monroe County and surrounding areas to experience and explore other cultures.

It is dedicated to letting people give expression to their common desire for friendship, goodwill, and cooperation toward a better world for all.

MIFA is a private, non-profit organization supported by a broad spectrum of the community. All MIFA members are volunteers who dedicate their own time for activities.

MIFA's main activity is an "Exchange Student" program with the Sister city of Monroe, Hofu, Yamaguchi in Japan. Our program is recognized and supported by JBSD (Japan Business Society of Detroit) and we receive donations from them. We are also supported by Rotary Club of Monroe and Aisin World Corp of America.

Sister City Program

What is Sister City?

Sister city is a form of legal or social agreement between towns, counties, oblasts, prefectures, provinces, regions, states, and even countries in geographically and politically distinct areas to promote cultural and commercial ties.

The modern concept of sister city, conceived after the Second World War in 1947, was intended to foster friendship and understanding between different cultures and between former foes as an act of peace and reconciliation, and to encourage trade and tourism.

Monroe city and Hofu city (Yamaguchi, Japan) formed the Sister City relationships in 1993 and started the "Exchange Student" program. MIFA organizes the program and coordinates activities with Monroe city.
